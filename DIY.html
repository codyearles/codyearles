I call this The Kun Block

Before Chrsitmas 2023...

I believe in attempting to make gifts rather than buying gifts. This little block came to be in quite a neat unfolding of steps. I think that little story is worth telling. I was thinking of what to give my Wife's dad as an Xmas gift. I really just started cutting a piece of wood in hopes that something meaning full would come up. The wood was an old Cherry tree that I guess fell 10 or 20 years previously, as the sap wood had completely rotted away. That takes time to happen. I was amazed at how strong the heartwood was after laying on bare earth exposed to rain, snow, and sun. I identified it as Cherry due to the sweet smell of the wood when cut. That exact same smell was on a tree truck that was present to hear fall. That tree was easier to identify as it had leaves and bark. They were neighbor trees. They must have known each other. Back to the gift, I was cutting the old truck into a post. From a post, it became 2" x 2" block. I sat with it relishing the sweet smell and smooth finish. The pattern of the grain and rings was captivating me. The block in my hand, that I had trimmed so much to make it straight, was proof of at least 30 cycles of life. The rings told of each year of life. Some were very tight and sparse while others were large and abundant. In that captivation my mind shifted and looked towards my bookshelf. There was "The Chinese Have a Word for It". I thought: "Huh. He like Chinese culture, maybe there will be some inspiration there." I scrolled through the Contents and stopped at "In Harmony with Nature". Perfect. Suits his him. I'll laser engrave that word on the block. The Kanji engraving came out very nice, I thought. The other side looks so nice, though. It would be nice to pick up and move around to enjoy this block as I'm doing now. I'll engrave another kanji on the other side! I went back to the book. The adjacent entry on the page was "Bad Days". Huh. Strange word. In English, pronounced as "Koon", spelled as "Kun". Ah! That was the same English pronunciation and spelling as "In Harmony with Nature". How interesting! Something that is almost an opposite is the same! The next page was the word was "Kung Fu", which is a generally known word as a form of martial arts. Meaning "the power of Chi" and with the same base as the words for "In Harmony with Nature" and "Bad Days". Huh. He practices Kung Fu regularly. Perfect fit. Immediately I went to engrave the other Kun kanji on the opposite side of the wood block, and decided to save the story (and the next kanji) for another block another day. I recommend researching the full meaings of these words. I hope you can see all the synchronicities in this story. I find it, humbly, quite profound.
